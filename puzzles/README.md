Here are **25 popular puzzles frequently asked in SDE interviews** (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Meta, Uber, etc.). These cover logic, math, probability, lateral thinking, and algorithmic reasoning.

---

# ✅ **25 Common SDE Interview Puzzles**

### **1. The Missing Dollar Puzzle**

The classic hotel bill puzzle with $30 → $27 → $29 confusion.

### **2. 3 Light Bulbs & 3 Switches**

Determine which switch controls which bulb when you can enter the room only once.

### **3. The 7-Liter & 5-Liter Water Jug Problem**

Measure exactly 4 liters using only 7L and 5L jugs. (Asked at Amazon)

### **4. The 3 Jugs Problem (8L, 5L, 3L)**

Measure exactly 4L or 1L using three jugs with no markers.

### **5. Wolf, Goat & Cabbage River Crossing**

Classic constraint-based river puzzle.

### **6. Two Doors & Two Guards (Truth/Lie Puzzle)**

Find the correct door with one yes/no question.

### **7. Prisoners & the Light Bulb Problem**

100 prisoners must determine when all have visited a room.

### **8. 12 Coins Weighing Puzzle**

Identify the fake coin (heavier or lighter) using only 3 weighings.

### **9. 9 Balls, One Heavier**

Find the heavier ball in exactly 2 weighings.

### **10. The Bridge & Torch Puzzle**

People crossing a bridge at different speeds with one torch.

---

### **11. Ants on a Stick Puzzle**

Multiple ants walk on a stick and reverse directions when they meet—find last fall time.

### **12. Poisoned Wine Puzzle**

Identify the one poisoned bottle among 1000 bottles using minimal test subjects (binary representation puzzle).

### **13. Trains & Fly Problem**

Fly travels back and forth between two approaching trains; find total distance.

### **14. Monty Hall Problem**

Probability puzzle involving switching doors.

### **15. 100 Doors Problem**

100 doors toggled multiple times—how many are open at the end?

---

### **16. Meeting Point Puzzle (Two People Walking)**

Two people start from opposite ends—where do they meet?
(Used to test understanding of relative speed)

### **17. Infinite Bus Seating Puzzle**

People with rules for seating (even/odd preferences, constraints).

### **18. Egg Dropping Puzzle (2 Eggs, 100 Floors)**

Minimum number of drops required in worst case.

### **19. The Circular Table Poison Puzzle**

Coins placed on a circular table—who wins first?

### **20. The Calendar Puzzle (Five Fridays)**

Find possible dates for a monthly pattern.

---

### **21. Knights & Knaves (Liar-Truthteller)**

One always lies, one tells truth—deduce facts from statements.

### **22. The Rope Burning Puzzle**

Two ropes that burn unevenly but take 60 minutes—measure 45 minutes.

### **23. The "Bridge Meets River" Puzzle**

Two cars start at different speeds—find crossing time.

### **24. The "Birthday Paradox" Puzzle**

Probability that 2 people share a birthday in a group.

### **25. The "Cheryl’s Birthday" Puzzle**

Logic puzzle made famous by Singapore exams and tech interviews.
